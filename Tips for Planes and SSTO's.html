Tips for Planes and SSTO's

Planes and Single Stage To Orbit vehicles (SSTO) are both fun and challenging to build, coming with both advantages and disadvantages.

Planes in KSP are very efficient, when using jet engines. Jet engines do have the advantage of low fuel burning to high thrust ratio but can't be flown above altitudes higher than 30km, due to the lack of air. Jet engines always require air even if you're in a sub-orbit. SSTO's are a mix between a plane and a rocket. SSTO's take-off like a plane via a runway and fly up as high as it can go via the jet engines. The rocket engine should then take over, allowing for non-atmospheric flight and orbiting.

Note: The SSTO has 2 jet engines then a rocket engine

To build planes always start with the basic then work to be behind the center of mass, not to far so that it still flips though. The only issues with this is if a plane or SSTO's uses all its fuel, when landing the center of mass will change. This thus results in the issue of the plane becoming uncontrollable and turning into a ball of fire. I also recommend set up action groups for engine start-ups and shutdowns.


Note the COL is behind the COM and the COT is inline with both the COL and COM.
