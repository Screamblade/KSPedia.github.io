Tips for Moon Landers

Moon landers are an essential part of any mission to other planets, even if it's a manned or unmanned vehicle. There are 4 key parts of the lander: The fuel tank and engines, the landing legs, the reaction contorl systems (RCS) and the main pod or probe core. The most basic of landers only need landing legs an engine and a probe core, the more advanced often come with docking ports, science experements and even carry rovers to be driven by the kerbal pilots.

Advanced moon landers can often have two stages. The first stage is meant for landing, it contains the main power and fuel for the mission. The second stage is designed to bring ONLY the crew and science back. Eletrical energy is often recquired to maintain contorland keep the reaction wheels going so make sure you strap some soler panels or Radioisotope Thermoelectric Generators (RTG's) to your vehicle. RTG's can provide constant eletrical production but weight a large amount.
